I'm John Gunnell, a graduate student in geomorphology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill's Department of Marine Sciences. I studied for my B.S. at the University of Michigan, working in Professor John Lehman's limnology laboratory. I moved on to UNC to earn my M.S. under Dr. Brent McKee, using radiotracers to reconstruct sedimentation rates in an incipient salt marsh. For my Ph.D., I continue to work under Dr. McKee, studying the transport and deposition of recent sediments.

Current Projects

  • Quantifying organic carbon burial in North Carolina salt marshes
  • Detecting fluvial sediment yield response to urbanization in the U.S. piedmont
  • Compiling soil characteristics of natural, restored, and degraded salt marshes
  • Relating continental-scale geomorphologic controls to coastal wetland abundance
  • Fingerprinting fluvial sediment sources using trace metals and naturally abundant radionuclides
  • Propagating uncertainty estimates in geochronological models
